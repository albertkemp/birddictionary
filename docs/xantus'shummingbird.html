Xantus's Hummingbird

Basilinna xantusii

The Xantus's Hummingbird is a mid-sized hummingbird. It's back is mostly emerald green, it has buff undersides and a rufous tail. They have a dark face with a bold white stripe over the top of the eye to the back of the head. The male's forehead is darker than the female's. It has a red beak with a black tip and browny wings with some moss green fluff just lining the patch of skin under the emerald and above the buff.

It feeds mainly on nectar from flowers and flowering trees using a long extendable tongue or catches insects on the wing. Its unusual name comes from the Hungarian zoologist, John Xantus de Vesey (Xantus János).
